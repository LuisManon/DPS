Gynecomastia

Gynecomastia is a condition that causes abnormal enlargement of the breast glands in men. Gynecomastia surgery, or male breast reduction, is a surgical procedure designed to reduce the size of male breasts by removing excess glandular and fatty tissue. This procedure can help improve the appearance of the chest and boost self-esteem. During the initial consultation, the degree of gynecomastia is assessed, and the most appropriate surgical approach is determined to achieve the desired results.

How to Prepare

  • Undergo a laboratory analysis or medical examination to assess your overall health.
  • Inform the surgeon about any medications you are taking and follow their instructions on which medications to discontinue.
  • Avoid alcohol consumption and stop smoking to improve healing and reduce risks.
  • Avoid taking aspirin, anti-inflammatory drugs, and herbal supplements to minimize the risk of bleeding.
  • Follow the surgeon's recommendations regarding diet and lifestyle before surgery.

What to Expect from the Procedure

  • A reduction in breast size, resulting in a flatter and more contoured appearance.
  • A recovery time that may include a few days of rest and limited physical activity during the first few weeks.
  • Possible swelling and discomfort in the first few days after surgery.
  • Visible results almost immediately, although the final appearance will be seen once the swelling has subsided.
  • Regular follow-ups with the surgeon to ensure proper recovery and address any questions or complications that may arise.

Surgery for gynecomastia is an effective solution for reducing the enlargement of male breasts and improving chest appearance. By choosing this surgery, you will be in the hands of professionals who will provide support throughout the entire process, ensuring that you feel comfortable and informed. With proper preparation and realistic expectations, you can expect results that will enhance your confidence and overall well-being.
